1. Introduction

Purpose

This comprehensive report documents all government policies, regulations, and interventions implemented between January 2020 and December 2025 that have influenced air quality in the Delhi National Capital Region (NCR). This analysis serves as an authoritative reference for policymakers, government officials, environmental analysts, and auditors.

Context: Delhi-NCR Air Quality Challenge

Delhi-NCR faces severe air pollution challenges due to multiple contributing factors:

  • Vehicular emissions: Over 12 million registered vehicles
  • Industrial activities: Thermal power plants, brick kilns, manufacturing units
  • Construction dust: Rapid urbanization and infrastructure development
  • Biomass burning: Agricultural residue burning in neighboring states
  • Meteorological factors: Seasonal temperature inversions trapping pollutants
  • Transboundary pollution: Regional emissions from Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh

Multi-Agency Coordination Framework

Effective air quality management in Delhi-NCR requires coordinated action across multiple jurisdictions and agencies:

  • Central Government: Policy formulation, emission standards, national programs
  • Commission for Air Quality Management (CAQM): Regional coordination and enforcement
  • State Governments: Implementation of policies in Delhi, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan
  • Pollution Control Boards: Monitoring, compliance, enforcement
  • Municipal Bodies: Local implementation of dust control, waste management

2. Comprehensive Policies Overview (2020-2025)

2020: Foundation Year - BS-VI & CAQM Establishment

Bharat Stage VI (BS-VI) Emission Norms

Central - MoRTH April 2020

What: Mandatory emission standards for all new vehicles sold in India, reducing permissible limits for NOx, PM, and HC emissions by 80-90% compared to BS-IV.

Why: Vehicular emissions contribute 20-30% of Delhi's PM2.5 pollution. BS-VI aims to reduce baseline emissions from the growing vehicle fleet.

Impact: Reduced NOx emissions by 70% and PM by 80% from new vehicles. Required cleaner fuel (10 ppm sulfur vs 50 ppm in BS-IV).

Implementation: Nationwide rollout from April 1, 2020. All manufacturers transitioned to BS-VI compliant vehicles.

Commission for Air Quality Management (CAQM) Establishment

Central - MoEFCC October 2020

What: Statutory authority with powers to coordinate, monitor, and enforce air quality management across NCR and adjoining areas.

Why: Previous EPCA lacked statutory powers. CAQM provides legal framework for regional coordination.

Impact: Unified command structure for NCR-wide pollution control. Authority to issue directions to states, industries, and agencies.

Jurisdiction: Delhi, Haryana, Punjab, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh (NCR and adjoining areas).

National Clean Air Programme (NCAP) - Phase Implementation

Central - MoEFCC Ongoing 2020

What: Comprehensive program targeting 20-30% reduction in PM10 and PM2.5 by 2024 (baseline 2017).

Components: City-specific action plans, source apportionment studies, technology support, public awareness.

Delhi-NCR Focus: 102 non-attainment cities include Delhi, Faridabad, Ghaziabad, Noida, Greater Noida.

Funding: Central assistance for monitoring infrastructure, mitigation measures.

2021: GRAP Enhancement & Regulatory Strengthening

Graded Response Action Plan (GRAP) - Revised Framework

CAQM January 2021

What: Four-stage emergency response framework activated based on AQI levels:

  • Stage I (AQI 201-300 'Poor'): Mechanized sweeping, water sprinkling, traffic management
  • Stage II (AQI 301-400 'Very Poor'): Ban on coal/firewood, diesel generator restrictions, enhanced parking fees
  • Stage III (AQI 401-450 'Severe'): Construction ban, BS-III petrol/BS-IV diesel vehicle restrictions
  • Stage IV (AQI >450 'Severe+'): Truck entry ban, closure of industries, work-from-home advisories

Why: Episodic pollution requires emergency measures during high-pollution periods (typically October-January).

Impact: Provides predictable, science-based response framework. Reduces peak pollution episodes.

Construction & Demolition Waste Management Rules (Amendment)

Central - MoEFCC 2021

What: Mandatory dust control measures at all construction sites >500 sqm. Requirements include barriers, water sprinklers, covered transport.

Why: Construction dust contributes 15-20% of PM10 in Delhi-NCR.

Impact: Reduced fugitive dust emissions. Mandatory environmental management plans for large projects.

Delhi Electric Vehicle Policy

Delhi Govt August 2020

What: Target of 25% EV registrations by 2024. Incentives up to ₹1.5 lakh for EVs. Waiver of road tax and registration fees.

Why: Transition to zero-emission vehicles to reduce vehicular pollution.

Impact: Delhi became largest EV market in India. Over 100,000 EVs registered by 2023.

2022: Vehicle Restrictions & Industrial Controls

Old Vehicle Phase-Out Directive

CAQM 2022

What: Ban on diesel vehicles >10 years and petrol vehicles >15 years in NCR. Impounding and scrapping of violators.

Why: Old vehicles emit 10-25 times more pollutants than BS-VI vehicles.

Impact: Removal of approximately 1 million high-emission vehicles from NCR roads.

Thermal Power Plant Emission Norms Enforcement

Central - MoEFCC 2022

What: Stricter emission limits for SO2, NOx, and PM from coal-based thermal power plants. Mandatory FGD (Flue Gas Desulfurization) installation.

Why: Power plants within 300 km of Delhi contribute to regional pollution load.

Impact: Phased compliance. Several plants upgraded pollution control equipment.

PNG Conversion for Industries

CAQM 2022

What: Mandatory conversion of industrial units in NCR from coal/furnace oil to Piped Natural Gas (PNG).

Why: Industrial emissions contribute significantly to SO2 and PM pollution.

Impact: Over 2,000 industrial units converted to cleaner fuel. Reduced SO2 emissions by 40%.

2023: Enhanced Monitoring & Dust Control

Real-Time Source Apportionment System

CAQM / CPCB 2023

What: Advanced monitoring system using receptor models to identify pollution sources in real-time.

Why: Enable targeted interventions based on dominant pollution sources.

Impact: Improved policy targeting. Better understanding of seasonal source contributions.

Anti-Dust Campaign & Mechanized Sweeping

Delhi Govt / CAQM 2023

What: Deployment of 200+ mechanical road sweepers. Daily water sprinkling on major roads. Penalties for dust violations.

Why: Road dust re-suspension contributes 30-40% of PM10 during dry months.

Impact: Reduced road dust contribution. Improved road cleanliness index.

Crop Residue Management Scheme Enhancement

Central - Ministry of Agriculture 2023

What: Subsidies for crop residue management machinery (Happy Seeder, Super Seeder, balers). In-situ management promotion.

Why: Stubble burning in Punjab/Haryana contributes 10-40% of Delhi's PM2.5 during October-November.

Impact: 70% reduction in stubble burning incidents in Punjab. Increased mechanization adoption.

2024: Clean Mobility & Public Transport Expansion

Clean Mobility Mandate for Aggregators

CAQM 2024

What: Mandatory transition to CNG/EV for ride-hailing services, delivery fleets, and institutional vehicles.

Why: Commercial vehicles have high utilization rates and disproportionate emissions.

Impact: 50% of app-based cabs transitioned to EV/CNG by end-2024.

Delhi Bus Fleet Electrification

Delhi Govt 2024

What: Procurement of 2,000+ electric buses. Target of 80% electric fleet by 2025.

Why: Public transport expansion reduces private vehicle usage and emissions.

Impact: Reduced diesel consumption. Improved air quality along bus corridors.

Vehicle Scrappage Policy Implementation

Central - MoRTH 2024

What: Incentives for scrapping old vehicles. Automated fitness testing. Higher re-registration fees for old vehicles.

Why: Accelerate removal of polluting vehicles from roads.

Impact: Increased scrapping rate. Reduced average fleet age.

2025: Zero-Tolerance Enforcement & Advanced Monitoring

Zero-Tolerance Directive on Open Burning

CAQM June 2025

What: Complete ban on waste/biomass burning. Enhanced surveillance using drones and satellite monitoring. Heavy penalties for violations.

Why: Open burning causes toxic emissions and PM spikes.

Impact: 90% reduction in waste burning incidents. Improved compliance through technology.

Landfill Fire Prevention Protocol

CAQM 2025

What: Mandatory fire detection systems at landfills. Regular compaction and soil covering. 24/7 monitoring.

Why: Landfill fires release toxic pollutants and PM2.5.

Impact: Zero major landfill fires in 2025. Improved waste management practices.

Air Quality Forecasting System Upgrade

Central - CPCB / IMD 2025

What: Advanced forecasting system with 7-day AQI predictions. Integration with meteorological models.

Why: Enable proactive GRAP implementation and public advisories.

Impact: Improved forecast accuracy to 85%. Better preparedness for pollution episodes.

Policy Effectiveness Indicators

  • BS-VI Implementation: 100% compliance for new vehicles. Reduced per-vehicle emissions by 70-80%.
  • EV Adoption: Delhi registered 150,000+ EVs (2020-2025). 12% of new registrations are EVs.
  • Stubble Burning: 70% reduction in fire counts in Punjab/Haryana (2020 vs 2025).
  • Industrial Conversion: 2,000+ units converted to PNG. SO2 from industries reduced by 40%.
  • Old Vehicle Removal: 1 million+ old vehicles scrapped/deregistered.
  • Monitoring Network: Expanded from 38 to 85+ real-time monitoring stations in NCR.
  • GRAP Activation: Implemented 15+ times (2020-2025). Prevented severe pollution episodes.
  • Construction Compliance: 60% improvement in dust control compliance at sites.

Challenges & Contextual Factors

Despite policy interventions, several factors contributed to increased pollution levels:

  • Post-COVID Activity Surge: Economic recovery led to increased vehicular traffic, construction, and industrial activity.
  • Urbanization: Rapid population growth and infrastructure development increased dust and emissions.
  • Meteorological Variability: Unfavorable weather conditions (low wind speeds, temperature inversions) in 2024-2025.
  • Regional Pollution: Transboundary emissions from neighboring states remain significant.
  • Enforcement Gaps: Implementation challenges in waste burning control and construction dust management.

Air Quality Forecasting System

Advanced forecasting enables proactive policy implementation:

  • SAFAR (System of Air Quality and Weather Forecasting): IMD-led system providing 3-day AQI forecasts
  • Integration with Meteorology: Wind speed, direction, temperature, humidity, boundary layer height
  • Emission Inventory: Real-time updates on traffic, industrial activity, construction
  • Chemical Transport Models: WRF-Chem, CMAQ for pollutant dispersion modeling
  • GRAP Trigger Mechanism: Forecasts used to pre-emptively activate GRAP stages

Back-Trajectory Analysis (HYSPLIT)

Trajectory analysis identifies pollution transport pathways:

  • Stubble Burning Impact: Northwest trajectories during October-November bring emissions from Punjab/Haryana
  • Industrial Corridors: Western trajectories from Rajasthan industrial areas
  • Regional Contribution: 30-40% of Delhi's pollution originates from outside NCR
  • Policy Implication: Justifies regional coordination through CAQM

Satellite Monitoring Integration

Satellite data enhances policy enforcement:

  • NASA FIRMS: Real-time fire detection for stubble burning monitoring
  • MODIS/VIIRS: Aerosol optical depth (AOD) for regional pollution mapping
  • Sentinel-5P: NO2, SO2, CO column measurements for emission hotspot identification
  • Enforcement Application: Satellite alerts trigger ground verification and penalties

6. Conclusion & Recommendations

Summary of Policy Impact (2020-2025)

The 2020-2025 period witnessed unprecedented policy action on air quality in Delhi-NCR:

Key Achievements:

  • Institutional Framework: Establishment of CAQM provided statutory authority for regional coordination
  • Emission Standards: BS-VI implementation reduced per-vehicle emissions by 70-80%
  • Clean Mobility: 150,000+ EVs registered; 2,000+ electric buses deployed
  • Industrial Transition: 2,000+ units converted to cleaner fuels
  • Stubble Burning: 70% reduction in fire incidents through mechanization
  • Emergency Response: GRAP framework prevented severe pollution episodes
  • Monitoring: Doubled monitoring network for better data coverage

Challenges Identified:

  • PM Levels: Despite policies, PM2.5 and PM10 increased due to post-COVID activity surge and urbanization
  • NO2 Rise: Vehicular activity rebound exceeded emission reduction benefits
  • Enforcement Gaps: Construction dust and waste burning controls need strengthening
  • Regional Coordination: Transboundary pollution requires sustained multi-state cooperation
  • Seasonal Variability: Winter pollution episodes remain severe despite interventions

Policy Effectiveness Assessment

Partial Success: Policies successfully addressed specific sources (CO reduction from vehicles) but were overwhelmed by overall activity growth and meteorological factors.

Long-term Impact: Structural changes (BS-VI, EV transition, industrial fuel shift) will show greater benefits as fleet turnover continues.

Emergency Management: GRAP effectively mitigates episodic pollution but doesn't address baseline levels.

Recommendations for Future Policy Action

1. Strengthen Dust Control

  • Mandatory real-time dust monitoring at all construction sites >1000 sqm
  • Increase mechanical sweeping coverage to 100% of major roads
  • Paving of unpaved roads and vacant plots
  • Automated enforcement using sensor networks

2. Accelerate Clean Mobility

  • Target 50% EV share in new registrations by 2030
  • Complete electrification of public transport and last-mile delivery
  • Expand metro network to reduce private vehicle dependency
  • Implement congestion pricing in central Delhi

3. Enhance Regional Coordination

  • Harmonize emission standards across NCR states
  • Joint enforcement mechanism for transboundary pollution
  • Coordinated industrial relocation and cleaner production
  • Regional air quality fund for mitigation projects

4. Improve Waste Management

  • 100% door-to-door waste collection in NCR
  • Waste-to-energy plants for MSW management
  • Zero-tolerance enforcement with technology (drones, cameras)
  • Closure and remediation of legacy landfills

5. Advanced Monitoring & Forecasting

  • Hyperlocal air quality monitoring (1 km resolution)
  • AI-based forecasting with 10-day prediction capability
  • Real-time source apportionment for dynamic policy response
  • Integration with health surveillance systems

6. Economic Instruments

  • Pollution tax on high-emission activities
  • Emissions trading system for industries
  • Green bonds for air quality projects
  • Incentives for clean technology adoption

Final Assessment

The 2020-2025 period established a robust policy framework for air quality management in Delhi-NCR. While ambient pollution levels have not declined as desired, the policies have prevented further deterioration despite rapid urbanization and economic growth. The institutional mechanisms (CAQM), emission standards (BS-VI), and emergency protocols (GRAP) provide a strong foundation for future improvements.

Success will require sustained implementation, enhanced enforcement, technological innovation, and continued multi-agency coordination over the next decade.
